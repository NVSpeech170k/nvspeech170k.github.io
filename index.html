Huan Liao1*, Qinke Ni1*, Yuancheng Wang1, Yiheng Lu1,

1 The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen

Abstract

Paralinguistic vocalizations‚Äîincluding non-verbal sounds like laughter, breathing, and lexicalized interjections such as ‚Äúuhm‚Äù‚Äîare integral to spoken communication. However, existing corpora rarely provide large-scale, word-level annotations of such cues, leaving them overlooked in conventional ASR and TTS research. Therefore, we present Emilia-NV, the first large-scale Mandarin corpus with word-level annotations for both lexical content and 18 paralinguistic vocalizations. It comprises 48k human-annotated utterances and 174k automatically labeled utterances (573h) The key contributions are: (1) a high-quality, fine-grained dataset of paralinguistic vocalizations that establishes a new benchmark for expressive speech modeling; (2) a paralinguistic-aware ASR framework that scales manual annotations to large corpora, enabling joint transcription of lexical and non-verbal content; (3) controllable zero-shot TTS with explicit token-level vocalization control, enabling natural and expressive synthesis.

Motivation

Existing corpora suffer key limitations: (1) most lack word-level alignment of paralinguistic and lexical content, preventing precise contextual modeling; (2) limited speaker diversity or recording conditions, with scarce Chinese speech coverage; (3) jointly annotated datasets either cover few paralinguistic categories or only at sentence level. To overcome these limitations, we present Emilia-NV, the first large-scale Mandarin corpus with word-level annotations for both lexical and PV.
Figure 1. The gap between conventional speech processing systems and paralinguistic-aware modeling. (a) Common speech datasets omit paralinguistic vocalizations, while Emilia-NV provides word-level, explicit annotations. (b) Conventional ASR ignores such cues; our NVASR jointly transcribes lexical and non-lexical content. (c) Standard TTS generates only text-based speech, while CV2@Emilia-NV supports explicit insertion of paralinguistic vocalizations for human-like synthesis.

Pipeline

Figure 2. Overview of Emilia-NV pipeline with paralinguistic-aware speech recognition and generation. (a) A word-level human annotated dataset of verbal and non-verbal vocalizations is first constructed. (b) NVASR model is trained to jointly transcribe verbal and non-verbal content. (c) NVASR is used to automatically annotate large-scale unlabeled speech. (d) The expanded dataset enables training CV2@Emilia-NV that explicitly renders paralinguistic cues.
